Q and A with Dr. "J"

Here are some answers to common questions we receive.

  • At what age should a child first see an orthodontist?

    Normall around age 8. At this age we just need to make sure there are not any major problems. Only about 5% of patients at this age need treatment. Of course, if you or your dentist feel something needs specific attention, please let us know.

  • What is the best age for braces?

    The best time is just after the baby teeth have fallen out, usually around age 11 or 12. Prior to this age, if there are no significant problems, we will monitor the development with short observation appointments every 6 to 12 months.

  • Why do I need braces?

    Dental health is the most important reason. If your teeth and jaws do not line up properly, tooth decay and gum disease can develop. Appearance is also an important reason for wearing braces. Many people have orthodontic treatment because they simply aren’t happy with the way their teeth look.

  • How long does braces treatment take?

    Orthodontics generally requires 18-24 months of active treatment (monthly visits) which is followed by a few months of “retention” guidance.

  • What about my regular dentist?

    You’ll need to continue to make regular visits (every 6 months) to your family dentist throughout your treatment. Periodic check-ups are important in order to prevent tooth decay and gum disease.

About Dr. Joseph

Dr. Joseph was born in Joplin in 1960. He attended Riverton (Kansas) and Joplin elementary schools and graduated from Joplin Memorial High School in 1978. Dr. Joseph was elected and served as the Memorial High School student body president in his senior year. After high school Dr. Joseph attended MSSC in Joplin. Then in 1981 he was accepted into the University of Missouri-Kansas City School of Dentistry. After receiving a Doctor of Dental Surgery (DDS) degree in 1985, Dr. Joseph completed a 24-month orthodontic residency at St. Louis University Medical Center. In addition to 2 years of clinical and didactic training in orthodontics, Dr. Joseph completed a Master’s Thesis based on his research in orthodontics, earning the degree of Master of Science in Dentistry (MSD) from the Graduate School of Saint Louis University in 1987.

Dr. Joseph also served as class president of his residency program at Saint Louis University Medical Center. Dr. Joseph has been a member of the American Association of Orthodontics, the American Dental Association and the Southwest Missouri District Dental Society, where he served as president in 1998-1999.

Dr. Joseph has been married for 13 years. He and his wife Vera reside in Joplin. They have four children who attend Thomas Jefferson School. Dr. Joseph has passed the Missouri Dental Board examination required to practice general dentistry and the Missouri Dental Board specialty examination required to be certified as a specialist in orthodontics.

Dr. Joseph has treated thousands of patients in Joplin for over 25 years
